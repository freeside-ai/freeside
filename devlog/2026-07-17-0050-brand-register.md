# Brand register codification (plan revision 10)

Plan §15 gains the settled brand register: the evolved tagline, the
proper-noun casing rule, the two-ground visual register, the signet-box
mark, and the accent grammar. All deciders below are the user unless
noted. This note is the durable home of the rejected-alternatives
record; the exploration board it distills is a session artifact, not a
project document.

## Tagline

Chose "the harness runs the agent; you hold the reins" over the prior
"the reins are yours" and over two explored variants, because "hold" is
a state rather than a transfer: Freeside's promise is control at rest
with judgment at gates, not continuous steering. Rejected: "Freeside
hands you the reins" (transfer verb oversells hands-on steering; the
named form was dropped for one-tagline-one-wording — where a surface
must self-identify, the wordmark or category line does that job) and
"Freeside is your saddle" (tack-to-tack antithesis and truthful, but a
beat slower to parse, and in the Count Zero loa register the human is
the horse; its state-over-transfer logic was kept). The category line
"an agent control plane" is unchanged and never decorated.

## Casing

Freeside is a proper noun, capitalized wherever prose can carry it,
including the wordmark; downcased only where the medium demands it
(freeside.ai, github.com/freeside-ai, `freesided`). Chose this over the
lowercase wordmark because all-lowercase is itself a tech-brand
mannerism — a small costume of the unix register — and the daemon's
honest lowercase carries that register where it is true.

## Two-ground visual register

Light surfaces arrive as Freeside (vellum ground #EDE7D6, bronze accent
#8F6B14); dark as Straylight (umber #16120E, tawny #C2912E). Modes
follow the viewer's system setting everywhere; the dichotomy assigns
meaning, never audience or surface. Chose this over per-surface theme
assignment (rejected: "public surfaces light, operator surfaces dark")
because good tooling follows the system, and terminals belong to the
dev's own preferences. The register reads the namesake's own
architecture: an engineered-daylight resort outside, the gothic villa
within; the third act (Count Zero's Boxmaker, making in the wreck) is
the self-portrait. Rejected as identity: neon cyberpunk (the costume,
not the book — the namesake offers resort daylight, gothic interior,
and dead-channel grey, none of them neon) and falconry as anchor (Free
as in Bird is a playable resonance; the identity must decode without
the org name).

## Palette grammar

Ages are for the materials of the place; artifacts keep their color.
Bronze/tawny is one metal in two ages; pool #6F9EA3 / mere #5D8489 is
the water note likewise; wax (#8A2D1C day / #9A3520 dusk, a legibility
lift only) is one color, seal moments only, because a commitment looks
the same in any room. Green is reserved for the semantic palette:
a pine accent was weighed and returned because success/go keeps its
color (the doctrine that pays for any accent: quiet success — shape and
neutral tick; failure keeps wax; attention keeps tawny; imported greens
from GitHub/diffs/terminals stay green). Also rejected as day accents:
tide (teal), tyrian (plum), leather (sepia); bronze won on unity with
tawny.

## Mark: the signet box

The plain chambered box — frame, one full-height divider, one arm, a
dot in the lower-right chamber — whose dividers, inlaid in the accent,
imply the maker's initial: the stem meets the ceiling and the frame's
own top edge closes the F in the eye. Nothing is drawn for the letter;
in one color it falls back to a plain box with no residue. Standing
rules, earned by rejection: never depict the agent (killed the
ring-and-dart); no small centered figures (killed ward-and-chamber and
spindle-and-room: anatomical drift at mark scale); the box stays sealed
(killed the warded box's opened wall); the letter is whispered, never
shouted (killed the wall-weight F); no drawn top arm in any form
(killed the edge-inlay, the wall-to-wall header, and the chamber-width
hanging bar); adapt the mark to small sizes, never swap artifacts
(killed the solid-tile seal-impression favicon).

Drawn spec (regenerates the asset set): specimen viewBox 0 0 100 100 —
frame rect 28,24 44×52 rx2 sw3.5; stem x=55.2, the golden division of
the 44-unit interior; inlay sw2.6; arm y=52 to the right inner face;
square stroke ends abutting frame inner faces, never overlapping; dot
r4.2 at (63.4, 63.8), centered in its chamber. Dusk cuts ×0.92
(3.2/2.4/r4.0) for irradiation. Icon cut viewBox 23 19 54 62, sw
4.5/3.0 (dusk 4.1/2.8), filling ~80% of its container. Favicons on
native pixel grids (32: 2px frame, stem x19; 16: 1px frame, 2px letter,
dot retired first) — pixel-snapping replaces optical compensation
there. One-color cuts share the geometry.

## Type

Source Serif for display and text (wordmark/headings/emphasis at
Medium 500 — semibold read over-heavy; body regular); IBM Plex Sans for
UI; IBM Plex Mono for evidence, a first-class register because
evidence, digests, and ledger lines are the product's truth. Rejected:
Alegreya (its calligraphy performed the manuscript — the costume test
for type: letterforms must perform no period or flavor; book
typesetting itself is mundane), Literata (idiosyncratic italics),
Archivo (thin at Regular, anonymous at Medium). Commercial upgrades if
ever wanted: Söhne, Berkeley Mono.

## Emotional target

Calm command — one temperament, two weathers (engineered ease by day,
kept gravity by dusk). Must not feel: accelerationist, neon-cyberpunk,
cute, enterprise-gloss, fear-led, acquisitive, or costumed (the
register is load-bearing, never decoration; every surface stays
mundane).

## Verification

Type and mark decisions were checked against headless renders of the
exploration board on both grounds (weights, italics, irradiation
compensation, favicon legibility at true 16/32px), not asserted from
description.

Revisit when: the app ships its first real UI (type at app scale; the
Plex/Source pairing re-judged there), a drawn wordmark is commissioned
(the mundane-surface route to a distinctive lockup), or the quiet-
success doctrine proves impractical in daily use (reopens the green
question).
